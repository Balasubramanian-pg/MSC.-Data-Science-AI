Using pre-attentive attributes in text is a powerful method to enhance clarity and direct reader attention, as text is the most fundamental form of presenting information. Without these attributes, a block of text requires more effort to scan and process.

### Enhancing Text with Pre-attentive Attributes

You can transform a standard, uniform block of text into an effective communication tool by strategically applying visual properties that the human brain processes automatically.

- **Color Highlighting:** By using color to emphasize a specific line or phrase within a paragraph, you draw immediate attention to that particular content.
    
- **Size Scaling:** Increasing the font size of specific phrases relative to the rest of the text acts as a pre-attentive trigger to signal importance or highlight key findings.
    

By leveraging these simple visual modifications, you shift the reader's experience from a slow, active search for information to a rapid, intuitive perception of the most critical elements.

To further enhance the effectiveness of text, several additional pre-attentive attributes can be employed beyond color and size. These methods help direct reader focus to essential information, such as rankings or sales data.

### Techniques for Emphasizing Text

You can use the following visual attributes to draw attention to specific phrases or data points within a textual body:

- **Outlining/Enclosing:** Drawing a border or enclosure around a phrase makes it stand out.
    
- **Emphasis Styles:** Applying **bold**, _italics_, or a combination of both can effectively capture the reader's attention.
    
- **Spatial Separation:** Creating a distinct paragraph around a specific phrase uses white space to isolate and highlight that information.
    
- **Underlining:** This is another tool to signal the importance of a particular line or phrase.
    

### Strategic Combination and Judicious Use

You can combine these features to maximize the visual impact on a body of text.

- **Layering Attributes:** For example, you can combine color, size, and bold formatting to highlight specific key findings, such as the highest or lowest ranked values or the greatest number of sales.
    
- **Text in Graphics:** When text is integrated into a graphic, adding color can further accelerate the speed at which a viewer identifies critical points.
    
- **The Principle of Judiciousness:** While these attributes are powerful, it is crucial to use them with caution. Overusing these features can undermine their effectiveness, so they should be applied judiciously to maintain clarity.

To maintain clarity and effectiveness in your communication, you must apply pre-attentive attributes in the correct proportions. Avoiding both overuse and underuse ensures that the basic features of your visual are not disturbed and that information flows smoothly to the audience.

### Building Visual Hierarchy

Another strategy to improve the processing of text is the creation of visual hierarchy. By utilizing a combination of attributes—such as size, color, and underlining—you can establish a natural order within the text.

- **Creating Order:** Hierarchy enables the reader to intuitively understand what to prioritize when reading.
    
- **Directing Attention:** By using a distinct color (e.g., blue) for key points and a secondary color (e.g., light grey) for the remaining text, you guide the reader to notice highlighted sections first.
    
- **Improving Readability:** This structured approach allows the reader to focus on the essential information to which attention is sought, before naturally transitioning to the other parts of the content.

To effectively guide reader attention through a body of text, you can leverage visual contrast and styling. By strategically applying these attributes, you can direct the reader to prioritize specific information.

### Strategies for Highlighting Key Information

- **Using Color Contrast:** If you intend to highlight 4 or 5 specific points, you can apply a distinct, prominent color to those segments while simultaneously lightening the color of the remaining text.
    
- **Natural Guidance:** This method naturally encourages the reader to pay more attention to the highlighted text, effectively creating a hierarchy of importance.
    
- **Combining Attributes:** You can employ a combination of techniques, such as **color**, **size**, or **underlining**, to emphasize critical aspects of your content and increase their visibility.
    

These methods ensure that the reader focuses on the parts of the text to which attention is sought, resulting in a more efficient and effective information flow.
